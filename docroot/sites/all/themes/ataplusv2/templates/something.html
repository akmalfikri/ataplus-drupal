Introduction

MyStartr Sdn Bhd (MSB) specializes in offering equity crowdfunding services, reward-based crowdfunding services and event activities in relation to startup development. MyStartr was started with reward-based crowdfunding in 2012. There are a lot of creative ideas and projects, yet funding is always a trouble for the creators and entrepreneurs.

Also, MyStartr has been organizing a well-recognized startups contest in the Malaysia Chinese-speaking business community, namely Dream Factory Startup Contest since 2017. In 2019, MyStartr is officially a licensed equity crowdfunding (ECF) platform recognized by Securities Commission Malaysia under the Malaysian capital market.

With a team of professionals, in less than 24 months, despite the Covid-19 pandemic, MyStartr has grown rapidly and become the second largest among the 10 registered ECF operators in Malaysia. In terms of market shares, MyStartr assisted 13 issuers to raise about RM 20mil in 2020, representing 15.6% market shares as compared with industry total fund raise of RM127.73mil. In addition, MyStartr facilitated the first RM 10 mil ECF project in Malaysia, which was achieved by Abletech Solutions Sdn Bhd (LOCKIN by Subplace) in August 2021.

MyStartr aims to assist start-ups and SMEs to grow further and has a mission of helping at least 5000 entrepreneurs to be successful by 2030. Hence, a full-fledged capital solution ecosystem which comprises three services is necessary: 1) reward-based crowdfunding services is to serve as ideation stage funding channel; 2) equity crowdfunding for accelerating capital, and networking activities with Venture Capitalists for future round funding; 3) and secondary market offers exit opportunities for investors.

In short, what MyStartr does is Raise Fund, Connect Investors & Integrate Resources

What We Do

The Challenge

For SMEs and startups:

  • There are a lot of potential companies with good business ideas that require financing for their development and growth. Yet, due to the reasons such as the company is new or doesn't present a strong revenue growth yet, the traditional financing options, such as bank loans are not available for them.

  • Private markets, such as venture capital firms usually have their own preferences, which set a barrier to entry.

  • They might also face the challenges of reaching out to potential investors due to the lack of market attention.

Our Solution

For SMEs and startups:

  • MyStartr is a full-service equity-based crowdfunding platform that curates high-quality deals in a variety of industries, including food, technology, beauty, healthcare, etc. A comprehensive onboarding SOP, associated with clear and actionable instructions is provided for the issuers to ensure that their ECF campaigns go smoothly, from the preparation to crowdfunding, as well as post-ECF investor relationship management activities, such as handling their own investors' group and quarter performance highlight to the investor.

  • Educating on the landscape and providing relevant incubator programs via the Dream Factory Startups Contest in order to help our customers as a catalyst for growing faster and further.

  • MyStartr also provides a reward-based crowdfunding service for new companies or startups to validate their product-market fit.

Why Are We Different

1. Focus on the Chinese market.

MyStartr differentiated itself by targeting projects and investors from the Chinese-speaking community. Most of the SME ownership in Malaysia are Chinese.

Table 1: Rough Breakdown of SME Ownership by Ethnicity.

Hence, providing a Chinese-based ECF platform which they are most familiar with is very crucial.

2. From zero to one support.

For SMEs, MyStartr guides and accompanies the successful applicants (fundraisers) throughout the whole process, from the preparation in the early stage, matters needing attention during the intermediate process, and every detail after the fundraising, ensuring that everything goes smoothly. We also provide an incubator program via Dream Factory Startup Contest in order to assist the entrepreneurs.

3. Many a little makes a mickle.

MyStartr puts more focus on retail investors as we believe in the power of the crowd. In the past, we have accumulated 3600 investors, 90% of whom are retail investors. The average number of investors per project is 130. It can be said that every project gathers the power of the masses.

4. Simple and convenient investment process.

Since most of the investors come from retail investors, our platform interface is integrated with the concept of e-commerce, making the investment process easier, more convenient and transparent.

5. A team of experts.

We have a team of professionals and are very committed as they strongly believe in ECF.

Our Revenue Model

  • Equity Crowdfunding

This business is to assist a company in hosting a fundraising campaign on an online platform from public investors with a small amount of investment each to get the business expansion funded. A standard fee of 8%, namely success fee, would be imposed on the company upon successful campaigns where it has successfully raised minimum target funding.

  • Reward-based Crowdfunding

Reward-based crowdfunding is a fundraising campaign for a project owner, either individual or businesses, who wishes to get a developing project (can be a product or services) funded by the support from the public with a small amount of investment each. Project owner (or fundraiser) would be charged a standard fee of 10% by the Platform upon successful campaign where it has successfully raised minimum target funding.

  • Event Management (Incubator)

The event management business segment is mainly focusing on a main annual contest, namely Dream Factory Startup Contest (DFSC), designed for startups to pitch their business idea and compete. This contest also includes a series of 2-months training programs that incubate the startups to be more ready to encounter market challenges. DFSC has been organized for the 4th years and there 2 main income sources for the contest which are sponsorship and crowdfunding success fees generated from first stage competition

Our Market Opportunity

Equity Crowdfunding is a new market in the financial industry where Securities Commission Malaysia (SC) introduced a regulatory framework in 2015. Since 2016, the ECF industry has hosted about 169 successful campaigns with a total fundraising of RM199.23mil, benefiting about 164 issuers. Most of the campaigns were completed over the last 2 years, especially 2020, representing the ECF market gaining traction compared with the earlier years. It is estimated to continue growing exponentially in the next 5 years.

In 2020, the market size of ECF was RM127.73 mil where it is still small compared with the size of the other market segment in the capital market.

Table 2: ECF in Malaysia

The market growth is mainly buoyed by (i) MSMEs which appear as the growth engine of the Malaysian economy, contributing about 60% of GDP, remains relatively underserved, with an estimated RM90 billion financing gap (based on Capital Market Master Plan 3); (ii) RM487 bil fixed deposits in 2021 (Bank Negara Malaysia Statistics) which could potentially be turned to ECF investment. Assuming 1% of conversion would mean a RM4.8 bil investment fund for the ECF market.

Given a strong ECF growth in 2020, we estimate a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 45% from RM383.2 mil in 2021 to RM3.6 bil in 2026.

Table 3: ECF Market Size Estimation.

Our Strategy

In Malaysia, there has been a gap between the financing needs and traditionalfinancing channels. As presented in the previous section, there is a huge opportunity and market. We believe that the demand for equity crowdfunding will increase as it gains more awareness. Over the next 5 years, the business strategy will breakdown into 3 major parts:

SHORT TERM

  • Scaling the ECF business segment by gaining a larger market share actively.

  • Building more strategic and corporate partnerships to attract more entrepreneurs to consider ECF for financing purposes.

  • Upgrading the current platform in terms of Ui/Ux and process automation, even an AI solution to accommodate and deliver a targeted number of projects, at the same time, providing a better user experience for the investors in order to get more conversion.

MEDIUM TERM

  • Developing a Secondary Market for investors to cash out.

  • Expanding our incubator service (via Dream Factory Startup Contest) by including accelerator activity - becoming angel investors of the promising projects incubated within our environment.

  • Helping more SMEs and startups to get more sales and funding through new product launching and special promotion on our reward-based crowdfunding service.

LONG TERM

  • Expansion into new global markets based on data and insights on key potential markets & interest in our offerings.

  • Going public through IPO.

Our Achievements

  • In 2020, our goal was RM 10 mil, but the final outcome was RM 20 mil.

  • Over 30 ECF projects have been completed, successfully raising over RM 50 mil with the support from more than 3500 investors.

  • In August 2021, we are the first ECF platform in Malaysia that completed a RM 10 mil ECF project - Lockin by Subplace.

  • There are currently 10 ECF platforms and MyStartr, which was established in less than 2 years, has become the second largest, accounting for 16% of market share in 2020.

Buy Back / Redemption

In the event of the occurrence of an RCPS Redemption Event (as hereinafter defined), if the Issuer has not exercised its right to convert the RCPS into Ordinary Shares pursuant to Paragraph 5 of this Section 6, the RCPS may be redeemed by 15 the Issuer on the Maturity Date, by giving a written notice (Redemption Notice) to the RCPS Subscriber. If the Issuer converts only a part of the RCPS held by the Subscriber, the remaining unconverted RCPS shall be redeemed in full by the Issuer on the Maturity Date. For the avoidance of doubt, the RCPS shall not be redeemable at the option of the Subscriber.

Early Redemption - The RCPS shall not be redeemed by the Issuer either in whole or in part, at any time before (a) the RCPS Redemption Event; or (b) the Maturity Date.

Liquidation Preferences

In the event of voluntary or involuntary liquidation, winding up, dissolution, cessation of business or return of capital of the Issuer (Event), the RCPS shall rank in priority to the ordinary shares. Upon the occurrence of an Event, the RCPS Subscriber shall, to the extent that there is capital available for distribution, be entitled to receive an amount equal to the aggregate of all accrued and unpaid RCPS dividend declared (if any) and the repayment of the Subscription Price.

Conversion Rights

Each RCPS carries the entitlement to be converted into ordinary shares of the Company at the conversion ratio of one (1) RCPS to one (1) ordinary share on or before the expiry of the Tenure. Upon the expiry of the Tenure, as an alternative to redeeming the RCPS on the Maturity Date, the RCPS may be convertible into Ordinary Shares by the Issuer, by giving a Conversion Notice to the RCPS Subscriber, within three (3) weeks prior to the Maturity Date. The RCPS Holder may prior to the expiry of the Tenure, at their sole option and discretion, opt to tag along the initial public offering (IPO) exercise of the Company, in which the RCPS shall be converted into ordinary shares in the Company based on the conversion ratio of one (1) RCPS to one (1) ordinary shares for the purpose of the IPO, with the conversion notice to be issued by the Company. In the event that the Company undertakes a trade sale of all the Companys ordinary shares, all the RCPS shall be converted into ordinary shares in the Company at the conversation ratio of one (1) RCPS to one (1) ordinary share for the purpose of the trade sale or acquisition if such trade sale or acquisition take place prior to the expiry of the Tenure.

Transferability

The RCPS shall not be transferable, unless with the prior approval of the board of directors of the Issuer (Board). Any transfer or purported transfer of any RCPS made otherwise than in accordance with Paragraph 2 of this Section 6 shall be void and of no effect and the Board and the Issuer shall have the powers and discretion to refuse to register any such transfer.

Voting Rights

Unless otherwise permitted under the Companies Act, the Subscriber shall have no right to vote at general meetings of the Issuer.
